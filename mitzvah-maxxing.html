// 01 — The Concept

Looksmaxxing optimizes the mirror.
Mitzvah Maxxing optimizes the soul.

Every "-maxxing" trend on the timeline is about grinding for status — looksmaxxing, gymmaxxing, moneymaxxing. Same energy, different stat. Mitzvah Maxxing takes that grindset and points it somewhere that actually matters: doing as much good as humanly possible. Locked in. For Hashem. For your community. For the people next to you.

The Flip

Same intensity. Different stat.

The looksmaxx grind is real — the discipline, the consistency, the "no days off" energy. We're keeping all of that. We're just changing what you're optimizing for. Instead of jawline and body fat percentage: tzedakah given, mitzvot performed, people lifted up, days you showed up when it would've been easier not to.

The Move

Hard mitzvah + daily mitzvah. Repeat.

Two reps. The hard one — going out of your way for someone, building community through force of will, the act that costs you something. And the daily one — the small consistent good deed that compounds into a life. Johnny did both. We're trying to do the same.

// 02 — The Method

Two reps.
Run them every day.

There's a hardmaxx and a softmaxx. You don't pick one. You do both.

// HARDMAXX
The Difficult Mitzvah

The one that costs you something. Time. Comfort. Pride. Sleep. The mitzvah that requires you to actually go out of your way — to show up when it would've been easier to scroll. This is where Johnny lived.

  • 01Visit someone in the hospital who has no visitors
  • 02Drive an hour to make minyan for a shiva house
  • 03Have the awkward conversation that someone needs you to have
  • 04Volunteer at the Shabbos meal you'd rather skip
  • 05Show up sober to the thing your friends are partying at
// SOFTMAXX
The Daily Mitzvah

The small reps. The compounding good. The mitzvah you do without thinking because you've done it 200 times. Soft doesn't mean easy — it means consistent. Daily is the cheat code.

  • 01Tefillin every morning, no exceptions
  • 02Tzedakah in the pushke before you leave the house
  • 03One genuine "how are you" — and actually listen to the answer
  • 04Learn one line of Tanya. One. That's the whole bar.
  • 05Smile at a stranger. Show them a Jew can be light.
// 03 — The Why

Johnny "the Jew" Ellis
Hoboken, NJ — z"l

Delivering Shmurah Matzah · Hoboken
"Save This Matzah." A mitzvah, in motion.

A few years before he passed, Johnny started showing up at Chabad Young Hoboken Jewish Center. He met Rabbi Shmuly and Rebbetzin Esta Levitin. He kept showing up. The transformation was deep and total — the kind of pivot people hear about and don't believe is real until they watch it happen in front of them.

Johnny had been living a different life. Then he found one that fit. He started chasing mitzvot the way he used to chase everything else — full intensity, full presence, full "I am here. Hineni." He called himself Johnny the Jew. He meant it.

"His identity changed forever. The spark was lit and the flame keeps growing every day." — CYP International, on Johnny

His best friend since fourth grade described him as "a great example of a person who took responsibility for themselves." That's the whole thing. He took responsibility — for his soul, for his community, for the next mitzvah in front of him. He did it loudly, joyfully, in public, with the kind of energy that made other people want to do the same.

Mitzvah Maxxing is for him. It's also for everyone who saw what he was doing and thought: I want some of that.
