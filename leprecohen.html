A Reverse Birthday Card — A Thank You for the Thank You

It is written — well, it's being written right now — that when someone blesses you on your birthday, you're supposed to bless them back. That's not Hallmark. That's Torah. The blessing isn't a transaction, it's a circuit. You completed it. The LepreCohen notices these things.

Thirty-five years walking this earth. The scroll has been unrolled at the Grove Street station, the beard is spiritually present, and the ancient words rise like rainbow smoke from parchment: you are seen, you are thanked, you are good.

Not "good" the way a card says good. Good the way Abraham welcomed strangers at the tent — with urgency, with real food, without asking first whether they deserved it. You showed up. That's the whole mitzvah. No triumphant orchestra. Just you, doing the quiet thing that actually matters.

May the One who blessed our ancestors bless you too — sideways, through humor, the way real holiness sneaks in when you're not performing it.
